# Legal Review Process

## What requires legal review

The following always require legal team involvement before any commitment can be made:

- **Customer paper** — When a customer wants to use their own MSA, DPA, or order form template instead of NorthstarCRM's standard documents.
- **Redlines to NorthstarCRM's standard documents** — Material changes to the MSA, DPA, or order form proposed by the customer or their legal team.
- **Custom DPA terms** — Non-standard data processing terms, including specific data residency requirements, sub-processor restrictions, or breach notification windows shorter than NorthstarCRM's standard.
- **Non-standard liability or indemnification terms** — Any request to modify the limitation of liability caps or indemnification scope.
- **Regulatory or compliance-specific addenda** — Documents related to HIPAA, GDPR, CCPA, or sector-specific regulations that add obligations beyond NorthstarCRM's standard DPA.

## What the chatbot can and cannot do

The chatbot can:
- Confirm that legal review is available during active deal cycles.
- Explain that custom legal terms require the legal team's involvement.
- Direct the prospect to the Account Executive to initiate legal review.

The chatbot cannot:
- Promise acceptance of any custom legal terms.
- Confirm whether a specific clause or term will be accepted.
- Provide a timeline commitment for legal review without AE involvement.

## Typical legal review timeline

- Standard NorthstarCRM paper (no redlines): no legal review needed; deal can close in 1–2 weeks.
- Minor redlines to NorthstarCRM paper: typically 1–2 weeks for legal review.
- Significant redlines or customer paper: typically 3–5 weeks, depending on the complexity of the changes and legal team availability.
- Customer paper with significant non-standard terms: may extend to 6–8 weeks.

## How to initiate legal review

1. AE confirms commercial agreement is in place (pricing, scope, plan).
2. AE submits the customer's document or redlines to the legal team via the deal management system.
3. Legal team reviews and responds with a redlined version or acceptance.
4. Negotiation proceeds between legal teams until both parties sign.

Legal review cannot be initiated before commercial alignment, as terms may change if the deal scope changes.
