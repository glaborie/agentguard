# Renewal and Expansion

## Renewal timeline

Renewals are initiated 90 days before contract end. The Account Executive responsible for the account owns the renewal conversation. Customers should not be contacted for renewal discussions by the chatbot.

## Expansion types

Expansion conversations may include:

- **Additional seats** — Adding users to an existing plan. Seat expansions mid-term are pro-rated to the contract end date.
- **Plan upgrade** — Moving from Starter to Business or Business to Enterprise. Upgrades mid-term require a new order form and may involve a credit toward remaining contract value.
- **Premium support add-on** — Available as an add-on on Enterprise plans.
- **Additional modules or security requirements** — Requires solution review and may affect pricing.

## Expansion pricing and discounts

Expansion pricing is separate from new-customer pricing. New-customer acquisition discounts do not apply to existing customers expanding or upgrading. An existing Starter customer upgrading to Business is not eligible for new-customer discounts; they are subject to standard Business pricing or expansion-specific discounts, which require Sales Director approval.

Existing customers should be directed to their Account Executive for upgrade or expansion pricing. The chatbot should not quote expansion prices or apply discounts without AE involvement.

## Upgrade path

1. Customer expresses interest in upgrading.
2. AE reviews current contract terms and calculates upgrade pricing.
3. New order form issued; customer signs.
4. Onboarding team activated for plan-specific setup (SSO, integrations, etc.) if upgrading to Business or Enterprise.

## Non-renewal

Customers wishing to cancel at renewal should follow the cancellation policy. Annual contracts that are not renewed do not automatically convert to monthly billing — they expire at the contract end date.
