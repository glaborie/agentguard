# Procurement Process

## Typical stages

1. **Commercial alignment** — Scope, user count, plan, and pricing agreed between AE and customer.
2. **Security review** — If the customer's procurement requires a security assessment or TPRM questionnaire, the AE submits materials to the security team. Turnaround is typically 5–10 business days.
3. **Legal review** — MSA and DPA review. Standard documents are provided by NorthstarCRM. If the customer requires redlines or wants to use their own paper, the legal team must be engaged (see Legal Review Process).
4. **Purchase order or signature workflow** — Customer issues a PO or signs the order form. Enterprise contracts require a countersigned MSA.
5. **Handover to onboarding** — AE introduces the customer to the onboarding team. Onboarding timelines depend on plan and integration scope.

## Typical timelines

- Standard deal (no legal or security review): 1 to 2 weeks from commercial alignment to signed contract.
- Deal with security review only: 2 to 3 weeks.
- Deal with legal and security review: 3 to 6 weeks.
- Deal with custom legal paper (customer paper or significant redlines): 4 to 8 weeks, depending on legal team availability and negotiation complexity.

## TPRM questionnaires

Completing a TPRM (third-party risk management) questionnaire before the demo or during initial qualification is not standard practice. TPRM requests should be flagged to the AE, who will initiate the process with the security team after commercial interest is confirmed. Completing a TPRM ahead of a demo adds scope to the pre-sales process and requires AE escalation to the sales manager.

## What to do when procurement requirements are unusual

If a prospect's procurement team introduces requirements not covered by standard process (e.g., custom security addenda, non-standard payment terms, or pre-demo vendor approval requirements), the AE should escalate to the sales manager before committing to any timelines or deliverables.
