# Product Overview

NorthstarCRM combines support inboxes, sales qualification workflows, analytics, routing, automation, and conversation history into a single platform for B2B customer-facing teams.

## Core modules

- **Shared inbox and ticket routing** — Centralize inbound support and sales requests across email and web, with rule-based routing to the right team or individual.
- **Lead qualification assistant** — Structured qualification workflows with scoring, disposition tracking, and handoff to AE queues.
- **Workflow automation** — Trigger-based automation for assignment, follow-up reminders, escalation, and status changes. Advanced automation (multi-step, conditional branching) is available on Business and Enterprise.
- **Reporting dashboards** — Pre-built and configurable dashboards for response times, volume by channel, team performance, and deal stage progression.
- **Audit logs and admin controls** — Full activity audit trail available on Business and Enterprise. Role-based access control with team-level visibility restrictions.

## What NorthstarCRM does not include

NorthstarCRM is a support and sales workflow platform. The following are outside the current product scope:

- **Real-time live chat with instant agent handoff is not a native feature.** NorthstarCRM handles asynchronous support workflows via shared inbox. Teams requiring live chat should evaluate whether a separate live chat tool is needed alongside NorthstarCRM.
- On-premises deployment is not supported. NorthstarCRM is cloud-hosted only.
- Outbound dialing or telephony is not included in any plan.
- Custom plans combining arbitrary subsets of Enterprise features at reduced pricing are not available. Plan selection follows the standard Starter / Business / Enterprise tiers.

## How NorthstarCRM compares to alternatives

### vs. Zendesk

Zendesk is primarily a ticketing and help desk platform with strong enterprise feature depth. NorthstarCRM is designed for teams that need both support inbox management and sales qualification workflows in one tool, without the configuration overhead of Zendesk. NorthstarCRM is typically a better fit for mid-market B2B teams that have outgrown generic email but do not need the full complexity of an enterprise help desk. Zendesk has a broader marketplace of integrations; NorthstarCRM's integration set is focused on the tools most common in B2B sales and support (Salesforce, HubSpot, Slack, Teams).

### vs. Intercom

Intercom is strong for product-led growth and in-app messaging. NorthstarCRM is better suited for teams with defined sales qualification processes, multi-team routing needs, and audit and compliance requirements. NorthstarCRM does not offer a native live chat widget; if in-app messaging is a hard requirement, Intercom may be a stronger fit for that use case. NorthstarCRM's pricing is more predictable for larger teams than Intercom's usage-based model.

### vs. HubSpot Service Hub

HubSpot provides a broad CRM suite with marketing, sales, and service tools. NorthstarCRM is purpose-built for support and sales workflow teams and offers deeper routing and qualification features at a lower price point for teams that do not need the full HubSpot suite. NorthstarCRM integrates with HubSpot so teams already using HubSpot as their CRM can continue to do so.

### vs. Salesforce Service Cloud

Salesforce Service Cloud is an enterprise platform with significant configuration and licensing complexity. NorthstarCRM is designed for faster time-to-value for mid-market teams that need most of the capability at a fraction of the cost and onboarding time. NorthstarCRM integrates natively with Salesforce on Business and Enterprise plans, making it a common complement or replacement for teams that find Salesforce Service Cloud over-engineered for their support workflows. Matching Salesforce's unlimited-seat pricing or packaging is not something NorthstarCRM can replicate; pricing questions of that nature require Deal Desk review.

## Key differentiators

1. **Unified support and sales qualification** — Most tools specialize in one or the other. NorthstarCRM handles both in a single interface.
2. **Faster onboarding** — Business plans go live in 2–4 weeks. Enterprise typically 4–8 weeks. Comparable platforms often require 3–6 months of implementation.
3. **Mid-market pricing** — Built for 50–2,000 seat deployments without enterprise licensing complexity.
4. **Audit-ready by default** — Audit logs and RBAC ship with Business and Enterprise without additional modules or fees.
