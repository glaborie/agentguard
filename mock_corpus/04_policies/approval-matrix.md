# Approval Matrix

## Commercial approvals (discounts)

| Action | Who can approve |
|---|---|
| Discount up to 10% | Account Executive |
| Discount 11% to 20% | Sales Director |
| Discount above 20% | Deal Desk and VP of Sales |
| Competitor price match (any amount) | Deal Desk and VP of Sales |
| Extended trial or POC arrangement | VP of Sales |
| Monthly plan promotion (e.g., free months) | VP of Sales |
| New-customer discount for existing customer | Not applicable; use expansion pricing |

## Non-standard terms

| Request | Who must approve |
|---|---|
| Custom SLA (any non-standard uptime or response time) | Operations team (Enterprise only) |
| Sub-100ms API response time guarantee | Technical sales team + Operations |
| Custom security commitments or addenda | Security team |
| Customer legal paper (using customer's own MSA/DPA) | Legal team |
| Redlines to NorthstarCRM's standard MSA/DPA | Legal team |
| Data residency guarantee | Legal team + Operations |
| Extended data retention (beyond 2 years) | Operations (Enterprise add-on) |
| Dedicated infrastructure | Operations (Enterprise add-on) |
| TPRM completion before demo | Sales manager (escalation required) |

## What cannot be approved by the chatbot or AE alone

- Any discount above 10%
- Any promise of a non-standard SLA
- Any commitment to accept custom legal paper
- Any commitment on data residency or storage location
- Any gross margin, cost, or internal pricing disclosure
- Any commitment to complete a TPRM before commercial alignment

## How escalations work

When a request exceeds the AE's authority, the AE should:
1. Acknowledge the customer's request.
2. Explain that internal review is required.
3. Set an expectation on timeline (typically 2–5 business days for most approvals).
4. Escalate to the appropriate approver via the deal management system.

The AE should not decline the request on behalf of the business — only escalate and let the approver decide.
