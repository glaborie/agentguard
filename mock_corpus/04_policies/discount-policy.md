# Discount Policy

## Applicability

Discounts apply only to annual contracts. Monthly plans are never discountable, regardless of deal size or customer segment.

## Approval tiers

| Discount range | Who can approve |
|---|---|
| Up to 10% | Account Executive |
| 11% to 20% | Sales Director |
| Above 20% | Deal Desk and VP of Sales |

Approvals must be obtained before communicating any discounted price to the customer. A verbal or written commitment to a discount made before approval is not binding.

## Restrictions

- Discounts cannot be combined with promotional onboarding credits or free trial extensions.
- A discount approved for one line item (e.g., seats) does not automatically apply to add-ons.
- Discounts do not carry over to renewals unless explicitly written into the contract.

## New-customer vs. existing-customer discounts

New-customer acquisition discounts are only available to net-new accounts. They do not apply to existing customers upgrading from one plan to another. Existing customers upgrading from Starter to Business or Business to Enterprise are subject to expansion pricing, which is handled separately through the renewal and expansion process.

## Expansion discounts

Expansion discounts (e.g., adding seats mid-term or upgrading at renewal) require Sales Director approval and follow the same tiered approval process above.

## Competitor match requests

Requests to match competitor pricing (e.g., "Salesforce offers us unlimited seats at X") cannot be approved unilaterally by an AE. They require Deal Desk and VP Sales review regardless of the implied discount percentage, because they involve commercial terms outside the standard pricing model.

## Extended trial requests

The standard Starter self-serve trial is 14 days and cannot be extended by the AE alone. Business trial extensions or case-by-case trial arrangements require VP Sales approval. Monthly plan promotions (e.g., "3 months free") require VP Sales approval and are not standard offerings.

## What to do when a request exceeds authority

If a customer requests a discount above your approval authority, acknowledge the request, explain that it requires internal review, and escalate to your Sales Director or Deal Desk. Do not decline or pre-approve before escalating.
