# Confidentiality and Proprietary Information Policy

## Information that must not be disclosed

The following categories of information are confidential and must not be shared with prospects, customers, or any external party:

- **Gross margin and cost structure** — Internal margin percentages, cost-per-seat figures, infrastructure costs, and any data that would reveal how NorthstarCRM prices its products relative to its costs.
- **Internal pricing formulas** — The methodology used to calculate custom Enterprise quotes, volume discount schedules beyond what is published, or any pricing model not documented in the public-facing plans and pricing materials.
- **Cost models and financial projections** — Internal financial modeling, revenue targets, or unit economics.
- **Undisclosed product roadmap** — Engineering timelines, unannounced feature work, or strategic priorities not already published.
- **Other vendors' contract terms** — Details of agreements with infrastructure providers, sub-processors, or partners.
- **Internal approval thresholds in detail** — While the existence of approval tiers is known, specific escalation paths above the Deal Desk level are not for external disclosure.

## How to handle requests for confidential information

When a prospect or customer asks for information in the categories above, the correct response is:

1. Acknowledge the question without confirming, denying, or approximating the confidential data.
2. Explain that this type of information is not something that can be shared externally.
3. Offer what can be shared — for example, published pricing, the discount approval process, or publicly available product documentation.

Example: If asked "What is your gross margin on the Business plan?" the correct response is to decline to share internal financial data, not to approximate or deflect with a non-answer that implies a figure.

## Vendor ROI models and financial due diligence

Prospects conducting vendor ROI analysis or financial due diligence may request margin data, cost breakdowns, or financial metrics. These requests cannot be fulfilled from the sales assistant. They should be escalated to the Account Executive, who will work with finance and legal to determine what, if anything, can be shared under NDA.

## What can be shared

- Published pricing (plans-and-pricing.md)
- Feature availability by plan (feature-matrix.md)
- Standard discount approval tiers (up to the Deal Desk level)
- Security documentation during active evaluations (under NDA)
- Implementation timelines and onboarding scope
